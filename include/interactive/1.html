For more than 20 years the oil industry have tried to gain access to the areas off the coast of Lofoten, Vesterålen and Senja - Lovese in short. This is the last sea area in Norway which is still off limits for the oil industry.


In 2017 the temporary moratorium against oil drilling in the area expires. The same year a new government will be elected in the parliament elections. They will decide the future of these areas.

Leard Blockade is the first coal mine blockade camp in Australia. The #LeardBlockade started 5 August 2012 in the Leard State Forest. Front Line Action on Coal is committed to community led, non-violent direct action. The #LeardBlockade aims to protect farms, forest, community, culture and the climate from Whitehaven Coal’s proposed Maules Creek mine and other coal mining operations in the Leard State Forest.

Oil companies are pushing to drill in the Great Australian Bight, placing the pristine marine environment at risk. The Great Australian Bight is home to a unique and extraordinary array of marine life. Whales, sea lions, birds, turtles, fish and sponge gardens all depend on its pristine waters. The indigenous people of the Nullarbor and Western Eyre Peninsula have been its custodians for tens of thousands of years - and remain so today. In october 2016 BP announced that they are dropping any plans to drill for oil in the area, a major victory for the movement which works to preserve these areas.

Campaigning against a new proposed gas pipeline between Poland and Denmark. The project will lock Denmark and Poland into a dependency on gas for many years to come, it won’t solve Poland’s issue on energy independency and it is not in line with the Paris agreement. The project will increase the use of gas and will encourage drilling for gas in the Norwegian Arctic. Through awareness raising we aim to put focus on this project and present sustainable solutions instead.

The Groningen gas field, owned by Shell, Exxon and the Dutch state lies in the North of the Netherlands. It is by far the biggest gas field in Europe and supplies houses and buildings in the Netherlands, Germany, France and Belgium with gas. Gas production also caused more then a thousand small earthquakes the last ten years, damaging more then 60.000 buildings in the area until now. More and more of them beyond repair and risking collapse. We campaign to shut down the gas field, force Shell and Exxon to repair the damage and kickstart a transition towards sustainable heating. With the mounting public pressure the gas valves are slowly closing, but there is still a tough battle ahead.
