The Lovese
Story

The Area

These are the areas of Lofoten, Vesterålen and Senja - Lovese in short. They are located in the North of Norway, and consists of 15 municipalities with around 62.000 inhabitants. The main industries here are fisheries, agriculture and tourism.

Lovese Drilling Zones

The body of water off the coast from Lofoten, Vesterålen and Senja is the epicenter of this story. These sea areas has so far remained untouched by the oil- and gas industry, but that could change this year.

A big oil producer

If we zoom out, we see that Norway has opened vast sea areas for oil and gas exploration. The sea areas outside of Lovese are some of the last areas in Norway which has been spared.

A slim continental shelf

One of the reasons for this, is the continental shelf. In these areas it is at its most narrowest - creating some very favourable conditions for fish, birds and sea mammals. Which again has helped sustain human settlements here for more than 6.000 years.

Oil and gas discoveries

But the same conditions has also created something else. Pockets of oil and gas has been found off the coast. The closest reservoirs are located only 15 km away from shore. These resources have been driving the controversial debate for the last 20 years.
