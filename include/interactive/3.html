The Area

These are the areas of Lofoten, Vesterålen and Senja - Lovese in short. They are located in the North of Norway, and consists of 15 municipalities with around 62.000 inhabitants. The main industries here are fisheries, agriculture and tourism.

Fish stocks

For thousands of years fish stocks like the Norh Atlantic cod has migrated to the sea areas outside of Lofoten, Vesterålen and Senja to spawn.

Bird colonies

The islands of Røst have the largest bird colonies in the European mainland. Here, hundred of thousands of sea birds have their nesting places and during winter time, even more sea birds migrate to these areas.

Some of the birds that come here include: puffin, razorbill, black-legged kittiwake, northern fulmar, common murre, common eider, king eider, european shag, great cormorant, great skua, long tailed duck and yellow-billed loon.

Many of the species that migrate here are critically endangered.
